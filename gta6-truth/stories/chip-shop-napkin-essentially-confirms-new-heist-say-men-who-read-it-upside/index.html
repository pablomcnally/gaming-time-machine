Chip Shop Napkin 'Essentially Confirms' New Heist, Say Men Who Read It Upside Down

A napkin from a Vice City chip shop has allegedly been interpreted as a full criminal operation by a group of locals with a biro and far too much confidence.

By By Nigel T. Haddock Tuesday, June 16, 2026 3 min read

Witnesses report that the napkin, stained with salt, vinegar and what appears to be the emotional weight of a Tuesday, contains three arrows, a circle and the words 'meet by bins'.

Experts in 'pattern looking' say this can only mean a multi-stage heist involving a forklift, a marina, and 'possibly a bloke called Darren'.

One analyst noted the napkin's corners were 'deliberately folded', while another admitted they may simply have been used to wipe chips off a wrist.

Theories are already circulating that the job will feature stealth, speedboats and at least one extremely overconfident getaway outfit.

Rock-solid evidence of this kind is best treated with caution, especially when the source is a crumpled takeaway napkin and the witness says, 'I knew it, the curry sauce does add up.'
