Map Corner 'Essentially Verifies' Three Secret Sunsets, Two Tramlines And A Bin Schedule With Attitu

A new round of map-based deduction has led fans to conclude the game world will contain more weather, more transport, and at least one bin collection with narrative depth

By By Penelope Wren, Map Enjoyer Thursday, June 11, 2026 5 min read

A fresh look at an unofficially imaginary map has revealed, in the eyes of analysts, "clear signs" of several things that are normal in cities but apparently thrilling in rumours.

The first clue is a suspiciously bright corner near the coast, which one enthusiast claims must mean the game includes three separate sunsets: "golden, dramatic, and the sort that makes you question your life choices".

Elsewhere, a thin line near a station has been declared evidence of tramlines, despite the fact that it could just as easily be a coffee stain, a ruler mark, or the universe having a laugh.

Particularly compelling, say the community detectives, is a tiny icon that may represent a bin collection timetable, prompting one man to declare the system "finally realistic" and another to ask whether bins now have lore.

Our conclusion is simple: the map says very little, fans say very much, and everybody remains heroically committed to staring at rectangles.
